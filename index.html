My Experience

I have been on a continuous learning journey over the past few years, exploring multiple areas of computer science and programming. I started with FrontEnd development, learning HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, building small projects to understand the fundamentals of web development. This early experience gave me a solid foundation in structuring webpages, styling user interfaces, and implementing interactivity. Later, I expanded my knowledge by studying Java at INTI University in Malaysia, where I learned object-oriented programming, classes, inheritance, and basic application development. Transitioning from web development to Java programming allowed me to appreciate the differences between frontend scripting and structured, compiled languages, and it strengthened my problem-solving skills.

Additionally, I am learning C programming and UNIX systems at INTI University, focusing on low-level programming, memory management, and command-line tools. Each of these experiences has contributed to a broader understanding of programming paradigms and technologies, preparing me for complex projects and problem-solving scenarios.

However, online learning, while flexible and accessible, presents unique challenges. The self-paced nature of Coursera and other platforms requires a high degree of discipline, organization, and motivation, as there is no immediate supervision or structured schedule. It can be difficult to maintain focus and consistently complete assignments on time without external accountability. Additionally, online courses sometimes lack hands-on support, which means troubleshooting and debugging often rely solely on community forums or personal research. Managing time effectively while balancing formal studies, personal projects, and online learning is another challenge. Despite these difficulties, I have developed resilience, independent research skills, and a proactive approach to problem-solving. I have learned to supplement online lessons with additional readings, experimentation, and project-based learning, which ensures that I not only understand theoretical concepts but also can apply them in practical contexts.

Overall, my learning journey has been rewarding and transformative. While online learning has its hurdles, it has taught me self-discipline, adaptability, and the ability to take ownership of my education. These skills will undoubtedly serve me well in future academic and professional endeavors, particularly in the rapidly evolving field of computer science and artificial intelligence.
